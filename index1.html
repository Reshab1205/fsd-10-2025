About MP

MP, or Madhya Pradesh, is India's second-largest state by area, known as the "Heart of India" due to its central location, with Bhopal as its capital and Indore as the largest city, featuring rich history, diverse wildlife (like in Bandhavgarh), UNESCO sites (Khajuraho, Sanchi, Bhimbetka), and significant mineral resources, governed currently by CM Mohan Yadav. Key Facts: Location: Central India, bordering Rajasthan, UP, Chhattisgarh, Maharashtra, Gujarat. Capital: Bhopal; Largest City: Indore. Area: Second largest in India. Population: Fifth largest, with over 72 million people. Government: Unicameral legislature; Governor: Mangubhai C. Patel; CM: Mohan Yadav. Highlights & Famous For: History & Culture: Khajuraho Temples, Sanchi Stupa, Bhimbetka Rock Shelters (UNESCO Sites). Nature & Wildlife: Bandhavgarh & Pench National Parks (inspiration for The Jungle Book). Rivers: Narmada, Chambal, Betwa. Minerals: Major source of diamonds, coal, copper. Economy: Agriculture is a major employer, with significant mineral wealth. History: Formed in 1956, reorganized in 2000 (Chhattisgarh separated). Major Cities: Bhopal, Indore, Jabalpur, Gwalior, Ujjain.
